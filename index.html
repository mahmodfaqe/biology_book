🧪 Histology - Study of Tissues

Microscopic examination of tissue structure and function

Slide 1
Areolar Connective Tissue
Components:
Fibroblasts
Collagen fibers
Elastic fibers
Ground substance
Macrophages
Mast cells
Plasma cells
📍 Location:
Under epithelial layers, around blood vessels, nerves, and lymphatics, between muscles and organs
⚙️ Functions:
  • Binding and Support
  • Cushioning and Protection
  • Nutrient Diffusion
  • Defense Mechanism
Slide 2
🔬 Adipose Tissue
Adipose Tissue
Components:
Adipocytes (fat cells)
Large, round cells
Central lipid droplet
Peripheral nucleus
Sparse connective tissue fibers
Capillaries
📍 Location:
Beneath the skin (subcutaneous layer), around internal organs (visceral fat), between muscle layers
⚙️ Functions:
  • Energy storage
  • Thermal insulation
  • Mechanical cushioning
  • Endocrine function
Slide 3
🔬 Reticular Tissue
Reticular Tissue
Components:
Reticular fibers
Reticular cells (specialized fibroblasts)
Supporting cells
📍 Location:
Lymph nodes, spleen, bone marrow, liver, thymus
⚙️ Functions:
  • Forms soft internal framework (stroma)
  • Supports cells in lymphatic organs
  • Filters body fluids
  • Provides structural integrity
Slide 4
🔬 Dense Adipose Tissue
Dense Adipose Tissue
Components:
Adipocytes (Fat cells)
Fibers
Blood vessels
Other cells: fibroblasts, macrophages, immune cells
📍 Location:
Subcutaneous layer, around internal organs, bone marrow (yellow marrow), breasts, between muscles and joints
⚙️ Functions:
  • Energy storage
  • Insulation
  • Cushioning and protection
  • Endocrine role (produces hormones)
Slide 5
🔬 Goblet Cells
Goblet Cells
Components:
Specialized epithelial cells (goblet/wine glass shaped)
Basal nucleus
Cytoplasm filled with mucin granules
Mucins
📍 Location:
Lining of the colon epithelium, interspersed among simple columnar epithelial cells in mucosal lining of colon and throughout intestinal tract, most abundant in large intestine (colon), especially in crypts of Lieberkühn
⚙️ Functions:
  • Secretion of mucus
  • Protection
  • Hydration and pH balance
Slide 6
🔬 Sweat Glands
Sweat Glands
Components:
Secretory portion surrounded by myoepithelial cells
Duct portion
Lumen
Surrounding tissue: dense irregular connective tissue, blood vessels, nerve fibers
📍 Location:
Found throughout skin, especially in palms, soles, and forehead (high density), armpits and groin (apocrine glands), embedded in dermis and extending into hypodermis
⚙️ Functions & Types:
  • Thermoregulation
  • Excretion
  • Hydration of skin
  • Pheromone release (apocrine glands)
  • Eccrine glands: cooling, found all over body
  • Apocrine glands: larger, armpits/groin, active after puberty
Slide 7
🔬 Salivary Glands
Salivary Glands
Components:
Serous acini
Mucous acini
Seromucous (mixed) acini
Intercalated ducts
Striated ducts
Excretory ducts
Connective tissue stroma
📍 Location:
Parotid gland, submandibular gland, sublingual gland
⚙️ Functions:
  • Secretion of saliva
  • Digestion of carbohydrates
  • Lubrication of food and oral mucosa
  • Antibacterial protection
  • Maintenance of oral pH and tooth health
Slide 8
🔬 Mammary Glands
Mammary Glands
Components:
Lobules containing alveoli (acini)
Duct system (intralobular and interlobular ducts)
Myoepithelial cells
Adipose tissue
Fibrous connective tissue (stroma)
Blood vessels and lymphatics
📍 Location:
Found in the breasts of both sexes, but functionally developed in females (especially during pregnancy and lactation)
⚙️ Functions:
  • Production and secretion of milk
  • Nourishment of the newborn
  • Endocrine responsiveness (regulated by hormones like estrogen, progesterone, and prolactin)
Slide 9
🔬 Small Intestine Epithelium
Small Intestine Epithelium
Components:
Columnar epithelial cells (tall, with oval basal nuclei)
Goblet cells (interspersed, secrete mucus)
Brush border (microvilli) on apical surface
Basement membrane at the base of the epithelium
📍 Location:
Inner lining (mucosa) of the small intestine (duodenum, jejunum, ileum)
⚙️ Functions:
  • Absorption of nutrients (via microvilli)
  • Secretion of digestive enzymes and mucus
  • Protection of underlying tissue
Slide 10
🔬 Pseudostratified Ciliated Columnar
Pseudostratified Ciliated Columnar Epithelium
Components:
Columnar cells with nuclei at different levels (gives stratified appearance)
Cilia on the apical surface
Goblet cells (secrete mucus)
Basal cells (stem cells near basement membrane)
Basement membrane
📍 Location:
Lining of the trachea and upper respiratory tract (e.g., bronchi)
⚙️ Functions:
  • Mucus secretion to trap dust and microbes
  • Ciliary movement to propel mucus upward (mucociliary escalator)
  • Protection and filtration of inhaled air
Slide 11
🔬 Buccal Smear
Buccal Smear
Components:
Flat, scale-like squamous epithelial cells
Central, round or oval nucleus
Thin cytoplasm
No visible intercellular junctions (in smear)
📍 Location:
Lining of the oral cavity (buccal mucosa). Part of the non-keratinized stratified squamous epithelium
⚙️ Functions:
  • Protection against mechanical stress and abrasion
  • Barrier to pathogens and harmful substances
  • Maintains moisture in the oral cavity
Slide 12
🔬 Oviduct (Fallopian Tube)
Oviduct (Fallopian Tube)
Components:
Tall columnar cells with oval nuclei
Cilia on the apical (free) surface
Basement membrane
May include peg cells (non-ciliated secretory cells)
📍 Location:
Lining of the oviduct (fallopian tube): including infundibulum, ampulla, and isthmus
⚙️ Functions:
  • Ciliary movement helps transport the ovum toward the uterus
  • Secretion of nourishing fluid for the ovum and sperm
  • Protection of the reproductive tract
Slide 13
🔬 Non-Keratinized Stratified Squamous
Non-Keratinized Stratified Squamous Epithelium
Components:
Basal layer
Intermediate (polyhedral) cells
Superficial squamous cells with nuclei
Basement membrane
Lamina propria (connective tissue)
📍 Location:
Inner surface of buccal gum (oral mucosa)
⚙️ Functions:
  • Protection
  • Maintains moisture
  • Acts as a barrier
Slide 14
🔬 Keratinized Stratified Squamous
Keratinized Stratified Squamous Epithelium
Components:
Stratum basale (basal layer)
Stratum spinosum (prickle cell layer)
Stratum granulosum
Stratum lucidum (only in thick skin)
Stratum corneum (cornified, dead keratinized cells)
Basement membrane
Underlying dermis
📍 Location:
Epidermis of skin (entire body surface): thickest in palms and soles
⚙️ Functions:
  • Protection against friction, pathogens, chemicals, and dehydration
  • Prevents water loss
  • Physical barrier
Slide 15
🔬 Simple Cuboidal Epithelium
Simple Cuboidal Epithelium
Components:
Cuboidal epithelial cells (cube-shaped with central, round nuclei)
Basement membrane
Visible lumen (in ducts or tubules)
📍 Location:
Lining of collecting ducts in the papilla of the kidney (renal medulla) of rabbit
⚙️ Functions:
  • Conduction of urine from nephrons to renal pelvis
  • Minor absorption and secretion
  • Protection of duct lining
Slide 16
🔬 Dense Regular Connective Tissue
Dense Regular Connective Tissue (Tendon)
Components:
Parallel bundles of collagen fibers (densely packed)
Rows of fibroblasts (tendon cells or tenocytes) between collagen fibers
Very little ground substance
No elastic fibers
📍 Location:
Tendons: which connect muscle to bone
⚙️ Functions:
  • Strong tensile strength for resisting pulling forces
  • Connects muscle to bone
  • Transmits mechanical force from muscle contraction to skeleton
Slide 17
🔬 Elastic Cartilage
Elastic Cartilage
Components:
Chondrocytes in lacunae
Dense network of elastic fibers (stained dark with special stains like orcein or Verhoeff)
Some collagen fibers
Perichondrium (outer fibrous layer)
Matrix (firm, flexible ground substance)
📍 Location:
External ear (pinna), epiglottis, Eustachian (auditory) tube, parts of larynx
⚙️ Functions:
  • Flexibility with support
  • Maintains shape of structures
  • Elastic recoil after bending
Slide 18
🔬 Hyaline Cartilage
Hyaline Cartilage
Components:
Chondrocytes in lacunae
Homogeneous, glassy (hyaline) matrix
Collagen type II fibers (not visible under light microscope)
Perichondrium (outer covering, except at articular surfaces)
📍 Location:
Tracheal rings, nasal septum, laryngeal cartilages, articular surfaces of bones, costal cartilages, epiphyseal plates in growing bones
⚙️ Functions:
  • Support with flexibility
  • Smooth surface for joint movement
  • Structural framework
  • Growth and development of long bones
Slide 19
🔬 Spongy Bone
Spongy Bone
Components:
Trabeculae (thin bony plates or beams)
Osteocytes in lacunae within trabeculae
Bone marrow spaces (between trabeculae, filled with red or yellow marrow)
Endosteum lining the trabeculae
No Haversian systems (unlike compact bone)
📍 Location:
Epiphyses of long bones (e.g., femur), interior of short, flat, and irregular bones (e.g., vertebrae, sternum), ribs, pelvis, and skull bones
⚙️ Functions:
  • Lightweight support
  • Houses bone marrow for blood cell production
  • Distributes forces and stress across joints
  • Metabolic role in calcium storage and release
Slide 20
🔬 Fibrocartilage
Fibrocartilage
Components:
Chondrocytes arranged in rows within lacunae
Abundant collagen fibers (Type I), densely packed in bundles
Sparse ground substance
No perichondrium (unlike hyaline or elastic cartilage)
📍 Location:
Intervertebral discs, pubic symphysis, menisci of knee joint, temporomandibular joint (TMJ)
⚙️ Functions:
  • Strong tensile strength
  • Shock absorption
  • Resistance to compression and stress in joints

  • Site of faster cell division
  • Site of slower cell division due to yolk
